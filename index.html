The Math Behind the Calculations

1. Total Surface Area ($A$)

Calculates the total external boundary of the rectangular incubator.

\[ A = 2 \left( H \cdot W + H \cdot D + W \cdot D \right) \]

2. Wall Conduction Loss ($Q_{loss}$)

Fourier's Law of steady-state heat conduction through the insulated walls.

\[ Q_{loss} = \frac{k \cdot A \cdot (T_{in} - T_{amb})}{\Delta x} \]

3. Evaporation Penalty ($Q_{evap}$)

Latent heat sink from eggs losing ~12% mass over 21 days (worst-case Day 1 condition where metabolic heat is 0).

\[ Q_{evap} = N_{eggs} \cdot 0.0095 \text{ Watts} \]

4. Total Heat Demand ($Q_{demand}$)

The absolute minimum heat required to maintain steady-state thermal equilibrium.

\[ Q_{demand} = Q_{loss} + Q_{evap} \]

Maximum Possible Demand

The general energy balance is:

\[ Q_{demand} = Q_{loss} - Q_{net} \]

where $Q_{net} = Q_{metabolic} - Q_{evaporation}$.

On Day 1 of incubation, the embryos produce no heat ($Q_{metabolic} = 0$). Therefore, the heater must compensate for both wall losses and evaporation:

\[ Q_{demand} = Q_{loss} + Q_{evaporation} \]

5. Physical Heater Target

Applies an engineering safety factor (2.5x) to handle rapid transients (door openings) and cold starts.

\[ Q_{physical} = Q_{demand} \cdot 2.5 \]

6. ANSYS Fluent Source Term ($q^{\prime\prime\prime}$)

Converts total Watts into a volumetric source. Assumes Fluent's default 1-meter 2D Extrusion Depth.

\[ q^{\prime\prime\prime} = \frac{Q_{physical}}{W_{heater} \cdot H_{heater} \cdot 1.0 \text{ m}} \]
